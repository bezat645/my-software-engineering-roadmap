About Me

Alongside a rigorous technical learning roadmap in full-stack engineering, I have a deep interest in climate-resilience strategies and international policy frameworks (NDCs). Achievements

Climate

Driven by a passion for intersectional problem-solving, I took on a pivotal role with Ershaye Connect, a youth-led civil society organization dedicated to agricultural resilience. Recognizing the fragmented state of agricultural data, I designed a comprehensive database management strategy aimed at centralizing data for smallholder farmers. The system seamlessly synchronizes climate patterns with crop cycles, providing actionable insights that help farmers adapt to unpredictable weather patterns. By bridging the gap between complex data structures and practical, boots-on-the-ground environmental action, this project stands as a testament to my commitment to building technology with a clear social purpose

Full-Stack

Transitioning from a programming enthusiast to an intermediate software engineer requires immense discipline, a challenge I met by designing and executing a demanding 14-month technical learning roadmap. While maintaining my academic commitments, I have systematically mastered core web technologies, building a rock-solid foundation in JavaScript and full-stack architecture. Currently expanding into the MERN stack , this structured blueprint is paving the way for my long-term transition into cloud engineering, containerization with Docker, and scalable B2B SaaS architecture. To keep this massive undertaking organized, I built a custom "Software Engineering OS" dashboard within Notion to track my learning objectives and daily coding milestones.

mechanics

Academic versatility has always been a cornerstone of my journey. I have successfully managed a dual focus, diving deep into advanced engineering mechanics—solving complex 3D truss analyses and polar coordinate kinematics—while simultaneously contributing to climate policy research.
